# Scenario Dungeon Rules

Every scenario must include:

- Scenario title
- Location
- Mood
- Recommended players
- Difficulty
- Story conflict
- Team goal
- Role list
- Emotion target for each role
- Hidden mission for each role
- 5 to 9 dialogue turns
- 1 to 3 key English expressions
- Possible rewards
- Result titles

## Good Scenarios

A good scenario has mild conflict, emotional tension, and a clear goal.

Examples:

- Cafe order goes wrong
- Friend cancels plans last minute
- Campus club interview
- Party introduction
- Mystery clue discussion
- Airport luggage problem
- Team project conflict

## Avoid

Avoid boring textbook situations:

- Generic ordering coffee with no problem
- Generic self introduction
- Memorizing vocabulary
- Grammar-only exercises

## Role Design

Each role needs:

- Identity
- Emotion
- Goal
- Hidden mission

Example roles:

Customer:

- Emotion: polite but a little annoyed
- Goal: explain the drink is wrong
- Hidden mission: ask the staff to check the order politely

Staff:

- Emotion: nervous but professional
- Goal: calm the customer
- Hidden mission: apologize and offer a solution

Friend:

- Emotion: light and humorous
- Goal: reduce awkwardness
- Hidden mission: help both sides relax
